Remento is the platform designed to help the families of people with dementia.

We designed an accessible and anxiety-free iOS app that tracks cognitive decline through daily, gamified interactions. Instead of a stressful clinical test, Remento acts as a simple routine that empowers families and carers with data about their loved ones memory decline to share with GPs. This was created in just 100 minutes at the 2026 UCC SDG Hackathon, my first ever hackathon.

I partipated in the hackathon alongside my teammates [Michal](https://michalszczepan.me) and [Jack](https://jackgarvey.me).

# Frontend

The frontend was programmed in Swift for iOS devices, allowing for a smooth, responsive, and highly optimized user experience tailored specifically to Apple hardware. A major design priority was accessibility, the interface used large, clearly labelled buttons, high-contrast colour schemes, and minimal on-screen clutter to ensure ease of use for elderly users, including those who may have limited experience with smartphones or technology in general. Navigation was kept simple and consistent throughout the app, reducing cognitive load and making it easier for users to build confidence over time.

Each test was split up into four parts. The first part of the test was examining episodic memory, usually the first to go in early stage dementia. It asked simple, multiple choice questions, such as "What colour is the grass?". The second part of the test looked at visual memory, presenting the user a pictogram and asking them to speak in as much detail as possible about it. The third part is similar to the second one except with an audio clip instead of the visual aspect.

A final part of the test is planned to be implemented at a later stage, mimicing the common clinical test of "drawing a clock" or "drawing a square" except on an iPhone.

Using all the data gathered from these weekly tests, we can map cognitive decline over time for elderly people.

# Backend

ElevenLabs API is used to process voice input and analyse vocal patterns. This way we could detect if the user was confused, angry, or unsure, while also verifying that any details they are giving are correct. Our algorithms would use the gathered information to generate a score, sending the gathered information in a PDF report to family members and GPs if allowed. This data correlates previous test results to current ones, and can inform the family member of ongoing cognitive decline
